About the Workshop

This workshop aims to explore how real-world advances in video generation increasingly rely on forward-looking evaluation frameworks and to collaboratively shape the next generation of high-quality video synthesis. Through a combination of invited talks, academic presentations, and expert discussions featuring leading voices from both academia and industry, the workshop bridges academic foundations and industrial insights across the modeling, evaluation, and deployment of video generation.

Topics of Interest

Video Generation & Control

Controllable video generation from prompts, layouts, motion trajectories, or camera behaviors. Incorporation of 3D, 4D, and physics-informed priors into generative video models. Modeling and evaluating narrative and expressive semantics in story-driven video generation.

Evaluation & Metrics

Evaluation of temporal, semantic, and causal coherence in generated video content. Development of human-aligned metrics for spatiotemporal consistency and narrative fidelity. Leveraging video large language models for interpretable and automated evaluation.

Benchmarks & Applications

Constructing datasets and benchmarks for long-form, multi-shot, and task-oriented video assessment. Feedback-driven and closed-loop video generation-evaluation frameworks. Real-world deployment in media production, education, virtual simulation, AR/VR, and robotics.

Submissions are invited, adhering to the main track NeurIPS format, with a page limit of 4 to 9 pages.
